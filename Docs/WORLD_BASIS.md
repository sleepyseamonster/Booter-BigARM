# Booter & BigARM World Basis

This document is the canonical baseline for the game's setting, tone, and core fantasy.

## High Concept

Booter & BigARM is a top-down, 2D pixel art survival crafting game set on a dead world called the Broken World.

The game is about endurance, deliberate movement, and living inside a hostile system rather than conquering it.

## Core Fantasy

- The player is a vulnerable survivor operating inside a vast, dangerous system.
- Movement, traversal, combat, and interaction are intentional and constrained.
- The experience should feel weighty and grounded, even from a top-down perspective.
- Preparation, attrition, and decision-making matter more than speed or power.

## The Broken World

- The world is a planet-wide canyon system carved into deep chasms.
- There are no oceans, rivers, rain, or plant life.
- The sky is permanently orange and the world reads in reds, rusts, and iron hues.
- The setting is procedurally generated and effectively infinite.
- Ancient ruins, broken war machines, and buried megastructures appear throughout the terrain.

## Survival Rules

- There is no water in the world.
- All liquid exists only inside living things.
- Human survival depends on algae.
- Settlements maintain algae vats.
- People carry personal algae reserves as the backbone of survival.
- Failure should come from accumulation: resource depletion, exposure, distance, and poor planning.

## Player Character

Booter is a professional desert survivor and metal scrapper.

- Booter is not a chosen hero.
- Booter is highly trained, but still vulnerable.
- Movement should have momentum, commitment, and positioning cost.
- Combat should be slow, intentional, and punishing when poorly positioned.

## BigARM

BigARM is an ancient war machine rebuilt into a mobile habitat, crafting platform, and AI companion.

- BigARM is a moving safe zone.
- BigARM is a crafting and upgrade hub.
- BigARM is a storage and recovery point.
- BigARM exists in the world simulation, not as a menu abstraction.
- The relationship with Booter evolves from hostility to reluctant cooperation to companionship.

## World Structure

- The game is a continuous infinite open world.
- There are no discrete levels.
- There are no mission-based segments.
- Progression is about surviving deeper and managing longer distances from safety.

The world should naturally create:

- Canyon structures
- Traversable paths
- Depth layers
- Ruins
- Encounter zones

## Core Loop

1. Travel through the world with BigARM.
2. Scout terrain and identify threats.
3. Salvage scrap, iron, and biological resources.
4. Return to BigARM to recover and prepare.
5. Craft tools, weapons, and upgrades.
6. Trade in settlements and outposts.
7. Plan and execute hunts.
8. Survive the consequences.

## Hunts

Hunts are large systemic encounters embedded in the world, not isolated boss arenas.

Targets may include:

- Ancient war machines
- Massive insectoid organisms

Hunts require scouting, preparation, environmental positioning, and risk evaluation.

## Tone

- Bleak, barren, and dead
- Quietly beautiful in its emptiness
- Minimal UI
- Environmental readability
- Sound and motion cues
- Systems over spectacle

The player does not observe the world. They operate within its systems.
